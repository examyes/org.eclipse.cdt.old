CDT Project Monthly Conference Call

September 2004
Minutes by Doug Schaefer

Sept 8, 2004

This is our regular monthly conference call where we can communicate through voice instead of e-mail. These occur usually the first Wednesday of the month.

The next call will be on Wed, Oct 6, 2004 at 1 P.M. See our Conference Call Page for connection details.

Participants

Red Hat, MontaVista, HP, Intel, Wind River, Texas Instruments, TimeSys, QNX, IBM Rational

Release Planning

CDT 2.0.2

CDT 2.0.2 is ready to put in the bag. It addresses a small handfull of severe bugs found in 2.0.1.

CDT 2.1

CDT 2.1 will target code freeze by the end of October. This means that RC0 will be built on Tuesday, November 2. We will spin release candidates weekly until we are convince there are no severe bugs remaining.

QNX is focused mainly on debugger work and upgrading the editors to take advantage of Eclipse 3.0 features.

Intel will contribute some of their work on managed build for 2.1 including the new model including file level settings and support for old extensions.

CDT 3.0

Everyone agreed that targeting "close to sync" deliver with Eclipse 3.1 in 2Q05 is acceptable.

Community Updates

QNX and Intel talked about their 2.1 deliverables. (see above)

MontaVista

Still considering the remote system model (ed. called target model at one point but this is a much better name). This will likely be a post 2.1 thing.

TimeSys

Waiting on 2.0.2 and the remote system model work.

TI

Interested in session for embedded development at EclipseCon.

Red Hat

Eclipse to be part of Update 3 for RHEL. Working on cdt-contrib plugins including hover help, oprofile integration, and a new ChangeLog plugin.

HP

Working on HP-UX port. Waiting for remote debugging story for servers.

Wind River

Announced intent to contribute to the CDT core in the area of refactoring support. The team from Salzburg, Austria will be participating.

IBM

Working on parser architecture improvements including definition of a complete AST. Also working on scalability and correctness.

Community Outreach

We have been contacted about writing a book about the CDT. Anyone interested in helping, please contact Doug Schaefer.
