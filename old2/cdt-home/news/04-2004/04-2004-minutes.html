CDT Meeting Minutes

April 7, 2004

Phone in meeting of Eclipse CDT stakeholders. Review progress to date and map out the future direction for the Eclipse CDT Project.

1:00 PM EST on April 7, 2004

 View the Agenda

 

Minutes
 

Here are the minutes of the CDT conference call.
 

Present:

QNX, IBM-Rational, RedHat, Intel, Altera, RealTime Innovations, MontaVista, Texas Instruments, Rockwell, TimeSys

** Apologies for not listing the individuals from these companies that attended the call, that notes sheet has been misplaced...

 

The following was discussed:

 

Update from participants:

 

QNX:

  1. Turning efforts to looking at standar make.
     
  2. C++ Class Browser coming along, some changes starting to trickly in
     
  3. Debugging work is continuing.
     
  4. The CDT registers view got abstracted out and pushed down into the Eclipse platform.
     
  5. Thomas Fletcher will look to contact the author of an eclipse core article on "How to Contribute a debugger". Please contact Thomas (ThomasF@qnx.com) if interested in the opportunity to contribute from a C++ perspective.

IBM-Rational:

  1.  Working to wrap up 2.0 features under development. Significant recent additions include Scanner Configuration, selection search, background search & build.
     
  2.  Have provided a drop of the UI to IBM Translation centers to get started on translations. Everyone's help to get strings externalized are appreciated, the various translated properties files will be contributed back into the project once they are available.
     

Redhat:

  1. Hover help, profiling & other plugins are ready for committing.
     
  2. Also working on a change logs plugin

Altera:

  1. CDT 1.2 based IDE engineering efforts wrapping up
     
  2. Coming up to speed on CDT 2.0, working on bringing plugins in line with a view to contributing (ideally in 2.0 but likely later to avoid churn).

Intel:

  1. Assisting in the managed build area, adding parser support. Patches expected soon.

Real Time Innovations:

  1. Working with CDT 1.x, following along with 2.0 developmnets.

MontaVista:

  1. Not many cycles devoted to CDT, still based on 1.x. Cycles not expected to free up until post 2.0

Texas Instruments:

  1. Eclipse 3.0 & CDT 2.0 based product is in the works, not ready for any announcement yet.
     
  2. There is a lot of buy-in at TI for contributing to Eclipse projects, will be crystalizing on areas for contributions. One known area for working on is Managed Build in order to support mixed-language requirements.

Rockwell:

  1. Deploying CDT 1.2 to internal projects, still working so resolve issues related to being able to contribute.

TimeSys:

  1. Some work being done on translation unit identification ...

 

CDT 2.0 Status:

  • No visible signs of progress in getting the committer permissions re-organized or getting the component inboxes created yet.
     
  • As we get closer to M9 (one month away), we need to start having some discussions on how feature development is progressing and make some decisions about which pieces to still keep in the release and which (if any) we need to start thinking about delaying until later in order to avoid risky churn past the M9 milestone. The late April timeframe was agreed to have such a discussion, and Kleo Hapitas took an action to poll for status from various teams to seed the discussion.
     
  • For items that we know are beyond the 2.0 timeframe, people are encouraged to create bugzilla plan items to start generating ideas and discussion. As an example, Doug Schaefer is planning to do some DOM prototyping and could get that ball rolling by capturing some initial information in a Bugzilla item & taking it from there...
