CDT 2.0 Kickoff Meeting
 

November 12, Ottawa Canada

Version 2.0 of the CDT was officially kicked off when contributors, users and other interested parties gathered in Ottawa on November 12 to share experiences, discuss plans and bounce around ideas on how to take the CDT up another notch. The large attendance in the meeting and the general enthusiasm of everyone certainly set the tone for great things to come from the CDT world!

Meeting Minutes (more like Meeting Highlights):

  • Round the room (and audio-conference bridge) for introductions

 

  • Steve Saunders, the Ottawa Lab director (IBM-Rational) welcomed everyone and expressed both his amazement and pleasure at the level of participation that this meeting had created, recalling how much smaller the 1.0 kickoff meeting, held a little more than a year prior, had been.

 

  • Sebastien Marineau of QNX gave a presentation on the current state of the CDT:
    • Presentation: State of the CDT
  • John Duimovich of IBM addressed the gathering with a high level update from the Eclipse Tools Project, talked briefly about how far the CDT sub-project had come and provided some guidance for what it would take to get the CDT to the next level: namely, active participation and contribution from all of the companies gathered for the meeting.

 

  • Erich Gamma of IBM demonstrated some of the changes to the Eclipse Platform and previewed some of those still to come in the 3.0 release of Eclipse, due in June 2004. This was not a canned demonstration that we could post here unfortunately...

 

  • Most of the companies participating in the meeting gave a brief presentation on their Eclipse/CDT efforts, goals and plans...

  • Kleo Hapitas gave a brief presentation proposing that the CDT project adopt the milestone based development process and end-game process utilized by the Eclipse Project. Following the presentation, all of the participants agreed that it made sense to do so and agreed to adopting it for the CDT 2.0 release.
    • Also discussed was the migration from the 2.1.x version of the Eclipse Framework to the new 3.0 version. It was proposed that the regular CDT 2.0 builds be cut over to the Eclipse 3.0 framework as soon as the M5 driver was available (Nov. 21). It was agreed that we needed to do that in order to start making use of the new features being delivered in the 3.0 release of Eclipse, even though it means we would not be able to maintain backwards compatibility with 2.x versions of Eclipse. There were no objections to attempting to cut over to Eclipse 3.0 as soon as the M5 driver is available.
    • A final point of discussion was the timeframe for releasing a maintenance release on the 1.2 stream. It was proposed that this be done late this year (December) or early next year (January), unless some participants had other needs. None were brought up, so the plan remains the same. A more concrete timeframe will have to be ironed out in the upcomin CDT conference call(s).

                            CDT 2.0 Organization (Milestone Based Development)

  • Brief Presentations were then given on the CDT Core, CDT Debugger, and the Embedded Roadmap. These presentations served as introductions to the breakout sessions that followed for the remainder of the afternoon.

                        CDT Core                CDT Debug            Embedded Roadmap

  • Following the breakout sessions, the participants gathered back together for a wrap-up. Many ideas and proposals for ways to move forward were discussed during the break out sessions, and at the end it was agreed that each of the proposals should be captured in a bugzilla entry (similar to how the Eclipse project team capture their proposals). A meeting will be organized in late November or early December to review all of the proposed work items and to see which ones can be committed to. It was further agreed that in order for a feature or enhancement to make it into the release, there has to be commitment to not only develop it, but also to test it and document it. That commitment does not necessarily have to come from the same team, but each of these areas need to be covered somehow.

 

  • Moving forward: A meeting to review all captured proposals will be set up for early December.
