CDT Developer Documentation

What does the CDT do and how does it do it...

Guidelines

Note: If you have any suggestions, feedback, or unanswered questions, please send them to the cdt-dev mailing list and we'll try to address them.

As the CDT begins to mature as a product and project and as the number of parties interested in getting involved grows, it becomes imperative that we lay out in writing the answers to two questions:

  • What does the CDT do and why?

  • How does the CDT do that and why?

The key factor in the above questions is “why”. As the CDT grows in functionality, it is important that the integrity of big picture is maintained. The CDT is built with specific goals in mind. These are honorable goals aimed at making the CDT the best C/C++ IDE on the planet (and off for that matter). These documents should hopefully convey what “best” means so that new developers can carry on the torch.

To help answer the two questions, the following two (or more) documents are created.

  • Feature Specification – describes what the CDT does. There are two main ways of describing features: first by describing the visible components that the users sees, second by describing how the user accomplishes specific tasks. This spec does both.

  • Design Specification – describes how the CDT does what it does. It also describes the architectural guidelines that should be followed by developers working on the CDT to ensure we can stretch the CDT in new and exciting (yet not really thought out yet) ways.
