CDT Case Studies

In order to understand the areas in the CDT that need the most attention, it is important to try the CDT on real projects. We will be taking a look at a number of different scenarios and assessing the performance and usability of the CDT in those scenarios. One of the benefits of the huge number of open source C/C++ projects, is that we have a lot of real projects to chose from. We also welcome teams that are using the CDT for commercial projects that may wish to share their experiences with the CDT.

The format of the case studies is a walkthough of a number of use cases. Problems that are found with the CDT are raised with comments in red. Note is made of the CDT build and publish date and the case studies will be updated as problems are fixed or new features introduced. The objective is to publish these studies as user community tutorials once most of the problems have been address. Of course, we expect users to find these and use them as guides, which is perfectly fine as long as they read the red comments as well :-)

Also note that by including certain open source projects, we are not necessarily endorsing their use or guaranteeing their applicability in any way. Now that the disclaimers are out of the way, here we go...

Irrlicth Game Engine
 This is an open source game engine written in C++. This case study walks though importing the source of the engine into a Standard Make project and walks through a tutorial using a Managed Make project.
